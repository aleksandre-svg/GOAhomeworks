Famous Works of Picasso

Guernica (1937)

Guernica is one of Picasso's most famous works, depicting the horrors of war and the suffering it causes. It was painted in response to the bombing of the Basque town of Guernica during the Spanish Civil War.

The Weeping Woman (1937)

The Weeping Woman is a series of paintings by Picasso depicting a woman in grief. It was created during the same period as Guernica and reflects the emotional devastation caused by war.

Les Demoiselles d'Avignon (1907)

Les Demoiselles d'Avignon is considered a groundbreaking work in modern art, marking the beginning of Cubism. The painting portrays five women in a brothel in Barcelona, with their forms broken into geometric shapes.

The Old Guitarist (1903-1904)

The Old Guitarist is part of Picasso's Blue Period, characterized by somber colors and themes of poverty and human suffering. The figure of the elderly guitarist is depicted in shades of blue and blue-green.

Girl Before a Mirror (1932)

Girl Before a Mirror is a famous painting from Picasso's later years, portraying his muse, Marie-Thérèse Walter. The work explores themes of identity, reflection, and inner turmoil.
