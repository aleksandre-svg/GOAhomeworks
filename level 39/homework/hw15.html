About NASA

The National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA) is an independent agency of the U.S. federal government that is responsible for space exploration, research, and scientific discovery.

NASA has been instrumental in studying our planet, other celestial bodies, and the universe. Their work helps us understand the potential dangers to Earth and how we can prepare for them.

What Dangers Could Earth Face?

Our planet faces a variety of threats, many of which are monitored by NASA:

  • Asteroid Impacts: NASA monitors Near-Earth Objects (NEOs) that could potentially collide with Earth.
  • Solar Flares: Powerful solar storms could damage Earth's communications, power grids, and satellites.
  • Climate Change: Changes in Earth's climate could lead to rising sea levels, extreme weather, and disruptions to ecosystems.
  • Global Pandemics: The risk of disease outbreaks, amplified by global connectivity, remains a serious threat to humanity.
  • Supervolcanic Eruptions: Massive volcanic events could cause widespread devastation and impact the global climate.
